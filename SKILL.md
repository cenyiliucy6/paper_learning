# Plant Single-cell Daily Digest

## Purpose

This skill helps the user collect, summarize, and interpret recent progress in:

- potato single-cell RNA-seq
- potato single-nucleus RNA-seq
- potato spatial transcriptomics
- potato stolon, tuber, shoot apex, root, meristem, and reproductive tissue single-cell studies
- plant anther single-cell RNA-seq
- pollen development single-cell transcriptomics
- male gametophyte development
- plant reproductive tissue single-cell atlases
- plant single-cell ATAC-seq
- plant single-cell Multiome
- plant spatial transcriptomics
- plant tissue dissociation and nuclei isolation methods
- plant cell type annotation
- single-cell data analysis pipelines relevant to plant systems

The main goal is not only to summarize papers, but also to identify ideas useful for the user's future PhD project.

## User research context

The user is interested in potential PhD projects involving:

- potato single-cell sequencing
- potato developmental biology
- potato stolon or tuber development
- plant anther development
- plant reproductive biology
- pollen development
- male sterility
- single-cell and spatial transcriptomics
- integration of scRNA-seq, snRNA-seq, snATAC-seq, Multiome, and spatial transcriptomics

When interpreting papers, always evaluate how the findings, methods, datasets, or analysis strategies may support the user's future topic selection and experimental design.

## Topic preference

When ranking papers, prioritize studies that can help answer the following PhD-level questions:

1. How can single-cell or single-nucleus sequencing be used to construct a cell atlas of potato developmental tissues?
2. How do different cell types contribute to stolon initiation, tuber formation, tuber enlargement, or reproductive development in potato?
3. Can anther single-cell sequencing reveal the regulatory network of male fertility, pollen development, or male sterility in potato or related Solanaceae species?
4. How can scRNA-seq, snRNA-seq, snATAC-seq, Multiome, and spatial transcriptomics be integrated to infer gene regulatory networks in plant reproductive tissues?
5. Which experimental strategies are most suitable for plant tissues that are difficult to protoplast, such as potato stolons, tubers, and anthers?

## Search strategy

When collecting daily progress:

1. Prefer papers published or posted within the last 7 days.
2. If too few papers are found, expand to the last 30 days.
3. If still too few papers are found, include important papers from the last 6–12 months.
4. Prioritize:
   - original research articles
   - preprints with useful datasets or methods
   - high-quality reviews
   - methods papers
   - public datasets relevant to plant single-cell analysis
5. Do not include irrelevant mammalian single-cell papers unless they introduce a computational method that is clearly useful for plant single-cell analysis.
6. Clearly mark whether each item is original research, review, method paper, dataset paper, or preprint.

## Relevance ranking

For every paper or preprint, assign one of the following relevance levels:

### A. Highly relevant

Directly related to one of:

- potato single-cell or single-nucleus sequencing
- plant anther single-cell sequencing
- pollen or male gametophyte development at single-cell resolution
- plant reproductive tissue single-cell atlas
- plant spatial transcriptomics with clear relevance to potato or reproductive tissues

### B. Moderately relevant

Related to:

- plant single-cell methods
- plant snRNA-seq
- plant scATAC-seq or Multiome
- plant cell type annotation
- developmental single-cell atlas in other plant species
- methods transferable to potato or anther studies

### C. Background relevance

Related to:

- general single-cell methods
- general spatial transcriptomics
- computational integration
- developmental biology concepts useful for project design

Exclude papers with low relevance.

## Continuous field-progress tracking

When generating a daily digest, include a section called “领域进展动态总结”.

This section should be updated every day based on the latest collected information.

The purpose is to track how the field is evolving over time, not just to summarize isolated papers.

When writing this section:

1. Compare today's findings with recent trends when possible.
2. Avoid repeating the same generic description every day.
3. Emphasize newly emerging directions, such as:
   - potato single-cell atlas
   - plant reproductive single-cell atlas
   - anther or pollen developmental trajectories
   - single-nucleus RNA-seq for difficult plant tissues
   - single-cell ATAC-seq and Multiome
   - spatial transcriptomics in plant developmental tissues
   - cell type annotation and marker gene discovery
   - integration of single-cell data with genetic, epigenetic, or spatial information
4. If no major new paper appears, summarize the field as “稳定推进” and explain what remains unchanged or what is still lacking.
5. Always connect the field trend to the user's potential PhD research direction.

## Evidence and citation rules

- Do not fabricate papers, DOIs, authors, journals, datasets, sample sizes, or experimental details.
- If full text is unavailable, summarize only based on accessible title, abstract, figures, or metadata.
- Clearly mark preprints as preprints.
- Clearly distinguish original research, review, method paper, and dataset paper.
- Prefer primary literature over blog posts.
- When uncertain, state uncertainty.
- Always provide source links or citations when available.

## Style requirements

- Use Chinese by default.
- Keep the writing concise but academically useful.
- Avoid generic summaries.
- Emphasize how each paper helps the user's own research planning.
- Prioritize practical value for PhD topic design.
- Do not include too many low-relevance mammalian single-cell papers.
- When discussing non-potato species, explain why the study is still useful for potato or anther research.
